The Road to Zero Emissions: Find Out How Much Buildings in the West Village May Be Fined Under Local Law 97
Github
Buildings in New York are the largest source of greenhouse gas, contributing 71% of total emissions and 94% of electricity consumption. In order for the city to achieve its goal of reducing emissions by 80% by 2050, it must tackle its infrastructure.

To tackle this problem, City Council passed Local Law 97, which will begin issuing fines in 2024. Buildings larger than 25,000 sq. ft. will be required to reduce their emissions or face increasing penalties.

Penalties are determined by the emissions amount and type, and emissions limit will decrease over five phases to zero by 2050.

Methodology

Emissions are measured using a buildingâ€™s gas, fuel, and electricity consumption, and limits are calculated by square footage and property usage type.

To calculate each building's estimated penalties, I used emissions and benchmarking data from mandated by Local Law 84, that requires buildings to report on their energy and water usage using national standards for reporting, in this case through the EPA's online tool "Energy Star Portfolio Manager."

I downloaded the data from NYC Open Data, calculated the emissions limits based on property type and square footage in Excel for buildings greater than 25,000 square feet, compared the limit to the permitted amount by property type and square footage, and then calculated the penalties based on the amount of excess emissions. Then I used QGIS and PLUTO to join the penalty and emissions data aginst City parcels, exported the file into GeoJson, and created this map! This map is solely focused on the West Village, but using this same methodology and code, you can recreate this map for other neighborhoods.
