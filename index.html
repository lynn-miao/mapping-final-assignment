The Road to Zero Emissions: Find Out How Much Your Building May Be Fined Under Local Law 97
Github
Buildings in New York are the largest source of greenhouse gas, contributing 71% of total emissions and 94% of electricity consumption. In order for the city to achieve its goal of reducing emissions by 80 percent by 2050, it must tackle its infrastructure.

To tackle this problem, City Council passed Local Law 97, which will begin issuing fines in 2024. Buildings will be required to reduce their emissions or face increasing penalties.

Penalties are determined by the emissions amount and type, and emissions limit will decrease over five phases to zero by 2050.
